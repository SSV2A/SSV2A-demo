Abstract

Audio synthesis from visual cues has wide applications in the multimedia industry. With the advancement of deep generative models, recent methods can synthesize high-quality audio signals given a silent video or image. For this vision-to-audio (V2A) generation problem, most existing methods assume a global video/image as generation condition. In reality, an audio clip is derived from sound sources only locally present in the visual cue. Can V2A generation also benefit from localized visual conditions? To answer this question, we propose a source-specific V2A generator (SSV2A) to synthesize audio clips conditioned on localized audio sources from a global visual cue. We separate the V2A task into a high-level semantic stage and a low-level waveform stage. In the high-level stage, SSV2A learns a contrastive Source Manifold that disambiguates each input visual source semantic. We then mix these manifold embeddings into a singular high-level audio representation in CLAP audio semantic space. In the low-level stage, a pretrained generator, AudioLDM, synthesizes the detailed audio waveform based on our generated audio semantics. We conduct extensive quantitative and qualitative evaluations and observe that our method outperforms state-of-the-arts in both generation fidelity and relevance. We further show that SSV2A achieves high generation control by omitting/adding source conditions and accepting cross-modal conditions from text/vision/audio. As a byproduct, we present a novel dataset, VGG-Sound Single Source (VGGS3), that offers 106K high-quality single-source audio-visual pairs. We also contribute a new metric, Matching Score, to measure generation relevance directly in the audio domain, which is shown to be more informative than the classic CLIP-Score in our experiments.
